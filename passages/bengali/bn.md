With over 100 million native speakers in India, Bengali is the second most spoken language in the country. It is the official language of West Bengal and Tripura and is also the national language of neighbouring Bangladesh. Bengali has a rich literary heritage, and Rabindranath Tagore, who wrote in Bengali, became the first non-European to win the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1913. The Bengali Language Movement of 1952 in erstwhile East Pakistan is commemorated globally as International Mother Language Day.
