Speed gives information; slowness gives understanding. A fast reading can gather facts, but a slow reading gathers questions.

The sentence changes when we return to it. Not because the words changed, but because the reader did.

Reading slowly is a discipline of attention. It refuses the market logic of urgency and chooses meaning over volume.
