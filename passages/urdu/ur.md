With over 50 million native speakers in India, Urdu is one of the 22 scheduled languages and holds official status in several states including Jammu & Kashmir, Telangana, Uttar Pradesh, and Bihar. Written in the Nastaliq style of the Perso-Arabic script, Urdu developed in the Indian subcontinent during the Delhi Sultanate period. It shares much of its grammar and basic vocabulary with Hindi, and its rich literary tradition of ghazal poetry and prose has produced iconic writers such as Mirza Ghalib, Allama Iqbal, and Faiz Ahmed Faiz.
