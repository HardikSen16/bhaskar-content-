With over 15 million native speakers, Assamese is the official language of the state of Assam in northeastern India. It is the easternmost member of the Indo-Aryan language family and serves as a lingua franca across much of the northeastern region. Assamese has a rich literary tradition, with early texts dating back to the 13th century, and the Buranjis, historical chronicles written during the Ahom kingdom, are among its most notable literary contributions.
