A river remembers every city it has touched, even after the maps forget. It carries silt, stories, and silence, layering time inside its current.

People often imagine identity as stone, but perhaps it is water. It bends around obstacles, finds alternate paths, and still reaches the sea with its name intact.

To inherit a culture is not to freeze it. It is to keep it moving with care.
