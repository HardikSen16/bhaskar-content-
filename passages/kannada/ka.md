With over 45 million native speakers, Kannada is the official language of the state of Karnataka in southern India. It has a literary history spanning over 1,500 years, with the earliest known inscriptions dating back to the 5th century CE. Kannada was recognised as a classical language of India in 2008, and its literary tradition includes the works of eight Jnanpith Award recipients, the highest number for any Dravidian language.
