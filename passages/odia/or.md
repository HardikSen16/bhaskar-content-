With over 35 million native speakers, Odia is the official language of the state of Odisha in eastern India. It was recognised as a classical language of India in 2014, and its literary tradition dates back to the 10th century. Odia is one of the oldest languages to have developed its own script, and the poet Sarala Das composed the first major Odia rendition of the Mahabharata in the 15th century, making it one of the earliest vernacular adaptations of the epic.
