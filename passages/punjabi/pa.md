With over 33 million native speakers in India, Punjabi is the official language of the state of Punjab. It is written in the Gurmukhi script in India, a script that was standardised by the second Sikh Guru, Guru Angad Dev, in the 16th century. Punjabi holds special religious significance as the language of the Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture of Sikhism, and it is also one of the most spoken languages in Pakistan and among diaspora communities in Canada, the United Kingdom, and the United States.
