With over 82 million native speakers, Telugu is one of the most spoken languages in India and the official language of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. Often called the "Italian of the East" due to the prevalence of vowel endings in its words, Telugu was recognised as a classical language of India in 2008. It has a literary tradition stretching back to the 11th century, with the Nannaya Bhattaraka's Andhra Mahabharatamu being one of its earliest masterpieces.
