Sanskrit is one of the oldest known languages in the world, with a literary tradition spanning over 3,500 years. It is the primary liturgical language of Hinduism and served as the lingua franca of ancient and medieval India. Though it has a small number of native speakers today, Sanskrit remains one of the 22 scheduled languages of India and was recognised as a classical language. It is the language of foundational texts such as the Vedas, Upanishads, and the epics Mahabharata and Ramayana.
