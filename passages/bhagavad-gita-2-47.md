*Yoga-sthah kuru karmani sangam tyaktva Dhananjaya, siddha-siddhau samo bhutva samattvam yoga ucyate.*

"Be steadfast in yoga, performing actions while abandoning attachment, remaining balanced in success and failure. This equanimity is called yoga."

This verse, one of the most profound in the Bhagavad Gita, encapsulates the essence of Karma Yoga—the path of action without attachment. Lord Krishna teaches Arjuna that the true practice of yoga is not merely physical postures or breathing exercises, but a state of mind where one acts with full dedication while being indifferent to the fruits of those actions.

The Gita teaches us that we have the right to perform our duties, but we are not entitled to the fruits of our actions. This principle liberates us from the chains of expectation and fear, allowing us to act with integrity and purpose regardless of external outcomes.
